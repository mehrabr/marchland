# MARCHLAND — Addendum P: The Theory of History We're Running
### If the tech tree is Whig history compiled, what is this — and where does our own engine betray or fall short of its philosophy?

**Extends:** Addendum O, which identified what the genre's mechanics assert about history. **Brief:** turn the lens on ourselves. A simulation *is* a historiographical text — its mechanics are claims about how history works, whether the designer intended them or not. So: what theory of history do MARCHLAND's mechanics actually assert, how faithful is the implementation to it, which divergences are justified scope, and what is genuinely missing?

---

## 1 — What the mechanics assert

Read the engine cold, as a historian would read a chronicle, and three commitments fall out.

**Materialism without teleology.** Outcomes grow from material and institutional conditions — calories, drill-days, road graphs, fiscal capacity, bond structures — and from nowhere else; there are no essences, no destiny, and no direction of travel. History in this engine is not going anywhere. This refuses Whig history, but note that it refuses a second teleology too: the stage-theory ladder (feudalism → absolutism → modernity as escalator) that even avowedly anti-Whig frameworks smuggle in. Our cultures are *portfolios*, not positions on a track; the Red Queen runs in circles, not up stairs.

**Contingency disciplined by mechanism.** Events are real and sampled; structures are real and computed; and the relation between them is the engine's one-line philosophy — *chance proposes, structure disposes* — operationalized by the ensemble. We can do the thing historians argue about being allowed to imagine: rerun the tape. Gould's famous thought experiment about replaying life's history to see what was contingent and what was convergent is, in this engine, a button. And the old methodological quarrel over counterfactuals — Carr dismissing them as a parlor game, the *Virtual History* school defending them as the only test of causal claims — gets a working resolution: counterfactuals are legitimate exactly when disciplined by validated mechanism, and illegitimate as free speculation. The ensemble is counterfactual history with receipts.

**Reflexivity: the writing of history is inside the model.** The chronicle that lies the way sources lie, the belief DBs, the patron's audit from imperfect knowledge, the tapestry's arrow versus the trace's sword — the engine simulates not only the war but the *record* of the war, and keeps the two distinct. Almost no historiographical school gets to demand this of its objects; we get it for free because epistemics were load-bearing from the first document. It may be the model's most original assertion: that a theory of history is incomplete without a theory of its sources.

## 2 — The kinship map

Name the relatives honestly, because the engine did not invent its philosophy — it compiled one.

**Braudel and the Annales school, structurally.** Braudel's three durations map onto the architecture with almost embarrassing precision: the *longue durée* (geography, climate, demography — the slow deep structures) is our substrate — the road graph, the seasonal supply field, the population densities that decide where armies can exist; the *conjoncture* (medium-term institutional and economic rhythms) is our receipts layer — fiscal capacity, drill cultures, doctrine, the career's decades; and *l'histoire événementielle*, the history of events Braudel famously demoted to surface foam on the deep tides, is the battle — the lattice's minutes. The tiered architecture of Addendum F turns out to have been the Annales school as a software diagram.

**But with Braudel's verdict reversed — which is the modern position.** Braudel dismissed the event; the engine *rehabilitates* it, carefully: events matter precisely when and only when structures sit near criticality, which is what the ensemble measures battle by battle. This is closer to William Sewell's "eventful temporality" — the post-Annales argument that events are not foam but the mechanism by which structures get transformed, that history is path-dependent and contingency is causally serious. The engine's structure-decided battles (Agincourt at 12/12) honor Braudel; its near-run battles (the square at 5/12) honor Sewell; and the *classification itself* — which battles were which — is the engine taking empirical positions in a historiographical debate, one scenario at a time.

**Tolstoy at the bottom, resolved at the top.** The battle layer is the second epilogue of *War and Peace* made executable — generals who do not control battles, armies moved by the sum of infinitesimal individual states, the "spirit of the army" as an aggregate of real men rather than a meter. But the commission frame puts a protagonist on top of this Tolstoyan substrate, and that tension had to be resolved rather than ignored: the station system *prices* the great man — the general matters as an anchor, as the maker of a few decisions at structural joints, and as a body the lottery can take, which is the modern middle position between Carlyle and Tolstoy. The career frame must never let the player become exceptional to the physics, and the design's instruments of humility (the audit, the capture, the ensemble's verdict on your famous victory) exist exactly to keep the protagonist *in* history rather than above it.

**Microhistory in the certificates; cliodynamics at the edges.** The death certificate — one man, one thigh wound, one uncovered arc, fully contextualized — is Ginzburg's microscope pointed at a battle: the conviction that the small, fully explained case carries general truth. And the validation battery makes us kin to the cliodynamics project — mathematical history tested against the record — though a deliberately humbler branch of it: we model campaigns, not civilizations, and every claim is scoped to what a battery can actually test.

## 3 — The faithfulness audit

**Where the implementation is more faithful than it had to be.** The reflexivity commitment is fully built (the chronicle/trace split, the Delbrück feature, the patron auditing from a belief DB). The anti-essentialism commitment has an automated enforcement mechanism (the receipts grep), which is more than most historiographical positions can say for themselves. And — the audit's best finding — **the validation battery is a historiographical instrument, not just an engineering one.** Recall the Hastings miss in Addendum E: the engine called the battle structure-decided at 12/12 when the historiography says near-run. That wasn't only a missing mechanic; it was *the model holding the wrong philosophy of history about one battle* — asserting Braudel where the record asserts Sewell — and the battery caught it. Every future scenario is therefore a test of the theory, not merely of the code, and the misses are where the philosophy gets corrected by evidence. Few theories of history submit to that.

**Justified divergences.** Two, both scope-honest. The *longue durée* is frozen: our geography, climate regime, and demography are static inputs per campaign, where Braudel's deepest layer moves — but it moves at a tempo a thirty-year career legitimately experiences as constant, so at commission scope the freeze is correct, with the explicit note that the grand-campaign mode inherits an obligation to slow variables (soil exhaustion, population recovery after plague and war, the supply field drifting across a half-century). And the model is *commander's-eye history*: the common soldier exists as appraisal, bonds, and receipts — far more than the genre grants him, but still instrumentally, from the command tent. That is what a war game scoped to a commander honestly is; the divergence is named rather than hidden, and §5 gives it one cheap, meaningful concession.

## 4 — The critical gap: events don't yet transform structures

Here is what the audit found genuinely missing, and the brief asked for elaboration, so: in the current design, structures produce events magnificently — the supply field shapes the campaign, the receipts shape the lattice, the lattice produces the battle. But the reverse arrow is weak. Battles write back *state* (casualties, prisoners, allegiance, priors) and they write back *standing* (the reform loop of Addendum O runs on victory). What they do not yet do is the thing Sewell's whole framework turns on and military history confirms on every page: **the event that breaks a structure's assumptions and forces its transformation.** Jena does not merely cost Prussia an army; it costs Prussia its *military paradigm*, and out of the catastrophe come Scharnhorst, Gneisenau, the staff system, the reformed state — the nineteenth century's most consequential military institution, midwifed by a defeat. Adrianople doesn't just kill an emperor; it ends an era's assumptions about what field armies can ignore. Our model can represent the defeat; it cannot yet represent the defeat *changing what is thinkable*.

The fix is a mechanic family, and it slots into the four-layer schema of Addendum O cleanly — call it **rupture**:

**Catastrophe as license.** The license layer (political permission for reform) currently prices change against standing, which makes reform a victor's luxury. History runs the other way as often: institutional transformation is cheapest in the wreckage, because catastrophe bankrupts the old paradigm's defenders. Mechanic: ensemble-extreme outcomes (a defeat the forecast called impossible, an army annihilated, a king taken) collapse license costs for reforms addressed to the failure's *diagnosed cause* — and the trace supplies the diagnosis, so the post-catastrophe court argues over the death certificates. The Reformer's golden hour is the morning after Jena, and the player who survives a disaster should feel the strange historical truth that they have never been freer to change everything.

**Doctrine shock.** When a battle's trace shows a repertoire defeated by something outside its assumptions — the first massed longbow harvest, the square that would not break, horses balked by a hedge of pikes held by townsmen — the event writes into *every observer's* belief DB as a paradigm datum, raising the perceived viability of the new method and lowering the old one's, for allies and enemies alike. This is diffusion (O) given its historical trigger: methods spread fastest not when published but when *demonstrated catastrophically*, and the engine can date each era's turning battles because the players fought them.

**Path-dependent scars.** Ruptures leave permanent marks on institutions, not just states: the army that broke at the bridge carries the bridge in its priors for a generation (cheap — priors exist); the patron who lost the host distrusts concentration for a reign (a license modifier with a memory); the reformed institution keeps its founding trauma's shape, over-fitted to the last war — which is the mechanism by which this engine could eventually model the most poignant pattern in military history, the army magnificently prepared for the war it already lost.

This family is the elaboration the philosophy demanded: with it, the engine holds *eventful* materialism — structures condition events, and events, at criticality, remake structures — rather than a one-way materialism that would, ironically, be its own kind of determinist heresy against the design's stated faith in contingency.

## 5 — Two smaller elaborations

**The multi-vocal Archive.** The commander's-eye divergence gets one concession that costs little and means much: the Page already renders multiple sources (chronicle vs trace); let it occasionally hold a third register — the soldier's letter, the village's parish record of its dead, the enemy chronicler's version of your famous day — generated from the same trace, scoped to a different witness. History from below as an *occasional document in the stack*, not a mode: the model stays a war game and stops pretending the tent's view is the only one the war produced.

**The battery as published historiography.** Since every scenario takes a position (structure-decided or near-run, and why), the validation battery should ship with its positions stated and sourced — which battles the engine calls contingent, which determined, and what the historiographical literature says — inviting the Student persona, and actual historians, to argue with the engine on its own evidence. The game as a falsifiable participant in the field it depicts.

**Verdict:** the model is *eventful materialism with reflexive sources* — Braudel's layers run forward by mechanism, Sewell's events restored at criticality, the counterfactual debate settled by discipline, Tolstoy underneath and a priced protagonist on top — and the implementation is faithful except for one missing arrow, now specified: rupture, the event that remakes structures. **Punchlist:** the rupture family (catastrophe-as-license with trace-diagnosed targeting, doctrine shock as belief-DB paradigm data, path-dependent institutional scars); slow variables reserved to grand-campaign scope; the multi-vocal Archive documents; the battery's historiographical positions published with sources.
