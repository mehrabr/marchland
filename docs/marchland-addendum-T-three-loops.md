# MARCHLAND — Addendum T: Three Loops on the March
### The campaign model under the logistics corpus — critiqued, fixed, re-validated, and universalized beyond medieval Europe

**Extends:** Addendum S's march model (v1), here subjected to the brief: hold it against the ACOUP logistics and marching material specifically, loop critique→fix until solid, and force the structure to universalize. Three loops were run. Each is documented as *the critique (sourced) → the fix (code) → the validation (ensembles)*. The model is now v2; the regression suite stayed green throughout.

---

## Loop 1 — "Where is the water?"

**The critique.** The logistics series is blunt: a man's daily load is ~1.36kg of food, but water adds 3kg more, and if it must be carried the daily burden jumps to 4.36kg and *operational range collapses into basically nothing* — in practice, **if local water wasn't available an army simply couldn't go there.** v1 had no water anywhere: its armies marched the Makran as comfortably as the Loire. For a model claiming universality this isn't a missing feature; it's a missing *continent* — every steppe, desert, and dry-season campaign on earth is gated by the variable v1 lacked.

**The fix.** Water becomes a route property (`water_ok`: the fraction of the route's camps that water), with carried water as a mode-limited buffer (~a day for land transport — the 4.36kg arithmetic enforced). A dry streak beyond the carried buffer kills directly and fast (thirst is a days-scale killer, unlike hunger's weeks), spikes fatigue, and degrades the camp tick. Geography, not the designer, now writes where armies can go.

**The validation.** Gedrosia, 325 BC — the canonical water catastrophe: 40,000 (column plus followers) across ~460 desert miles, wells at barely six camps in ten, late-summer heat. The ensemble: dead 30,400 (76% — sitting exactly on Plutarch's three-quarters tradition, flagged C-grade since modern scholarship suspects the ancient figure runs high), survivors arriving as a strung-out remnant at fatigue 1.0 and cohesion 0.0 — the shattered column the sources describe, generated by two numbers: `water_ok=0.62`, `heat=1.5`.

## Loop 2 — "The train doesn't eat"

**The critique.** v1's supply was an enum and a dice roll. The corpus's whole center is an *equation*: the worked consular army — 19,200 effectives, 4,000 noncombatants, 9,800 animals — needs **61,850kg of food and hard fodder daily**, can take roughly **10% of available food along its line of march** while moving, sweeps ~240 square miles a day with its cavalry screens, against a countryside holding ~250kg per rural inhabitant — *and the countryside is seasonal*, peaking at harvest and a quarter to a third gone by October. The animals are half the problem: they need green fodder (grazing, which costs marching time) and dry fodder (the harvest's barley and oats), both seasonal. And the transport itself is a technology table with hard trade-offs — an ox cart hauls 540kg at 2mph for five hours; a pack horse 110kg at 4mph for eight. v1 modeled none of this: no consumption-vs-capacity balance, no seasons, no modes, no depletion.

**The fix.** The universal carrying equation, as the model's new spine: daily need = men×1.4kg + Σ(carriers × their own consumption, offset by grazing when the season is green and the column pays the time tax); intake = depot deliveries + foraging, where foraging = swept area (screens × pace × dispersal) × land density × season factor × the 10%-on-the-move take, scaled by cohesion (a disordered army forages badly); the stock integrates the difference, and starvation is the integral going negative — the Wellington multiplier then triples the straggle rate and eats cohesion before it kills anyone. Transport is now a mode table (`porter / pack / ox / wagon`, each with payload, appetite, speed cap, and column-length cost at the ACW's ~80 wagons to the mile), mixable per army — which is the universalization hinge: a Han porter train, a Mughal grain-bazaar on oxen, and a Union wagon park are *rows in the same table*, not different models.

**The validation.** Two new ensembles bracketing the equation from both ends, plus the regression. **Sherman, 1864** — the modern test: 62,000 men cut loose from depots into post-harvest Georgia on four parallel road-columns with wide screens — arrives in 24 days (historical ~26) with 4.2% total wastage, cohesion 1.0, sixteen days of food in hand *taken entirely from the land*: forage mode succeeding for receipt reasons (density 22, roads 4, screens 25 miles), the march-divided geometry reproduced by the congestion math rather than asserted. **The Niemen, 1812** — the equation failing at scale: 286,000 men whose daily need can never be met by a 10% take on thin Lithuanian density, prepared stocks burning out in under a month, then the entropy storm — 62% of the central mass gone by mile 520 with battle contributing nothing, the deserter-marauder continuum dominating, which is the left half of the Minard graph emerging from arithmetic. And the **regression stayed green**: Agincourt still arrives in 17 of 17 days at 7,787 effective, fatigue 0.55, cohesion 0.40; Marlborough still delivers his 2.8%-wastage miracle, now *via* scheduled depot intake instead of a magic enum.

## Loop 3 — "Your constants are wearing a culture"

**The critique.** Self-inflicted, per the project's own Addendum R discipline: v1 blended physics with period assumptions — wagon-shaped column math baked into a universal constant, no seasons, supply as European categories, and no audit of which numbers were bodies and which were institutions. A model that universalizes must be able to say, for every constant, *who would have to change for this number to change.*

**The fix.** The constants registry, split and tagged in the source:

```
CLASS A — bodies (every human, every era):
  1.4kg grain/day · 3kg water/day · 2.5mph of feet · 10h of light ·
  thirst lethality · the fatigue and straggle curves
CLASS B — transport technology (receipts; purchasable, mixable):
  the mode table: porter/pack/ox/wagon — payload, appetite, speed, column cost
CLASS C — geography and season (the world writes these):
  land density · season factor · water profile · road count and quality · heat
CLASS D — institutions (drill, custom, administration):
  officer/provost capacity · discipline regime · desert_share customs ·
  depot scheduling · home_pull's meaning
```

The day-pipeline itself — *equation → water → column → fatigue/cohesion → three outflows → camp tick* — is the universal schema; everything cultural enters as a row of data. A Roman column, a Sengoku han's march, a Sahelian dry-season campaign, and Sherman's bummers are now configurations, and the registry makes the claim auditable the same way the battle layer's receipts grep does.

**The validation.** The spread of the five passing ensembles *is* the test: one schema producing the 1415 shadow-march, the 1704 depot miracle, the 1812 starvation storm, the 325 BC thirst catastrophe, and the 1864 forage triumph — five centuries, three continents-worth of logistics regimes, zero per-scenario rule changes.
